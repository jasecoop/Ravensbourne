  • ‘Making Death Subtle’ addresses individual grief. To allow a family to grieve individually, I have designed a set of contemporary Urns, in the form of candleholders. The internal capsule to each candleholder fluctuates in the volume of ‘remains’ it contains. The difference in volume depends entirely on the dynamics of the family tree.

    For example; Grandad has passed away. Grandma is still alive. They both had three children and three grandchildren. Grandma is entitled to the largest quantity of remains to keep, as she was married to Grandad for 69 years. The ages of the three children are 65, 62 and 58. The quantity of remains shared depends of their age. Again, this arrangement of dividing the remains continues throughout the family tree to the grandchildren.

    These objects are available in the following stone; Granite, Limestone, Marble and Quartz. The choice of stone lies with each member within the family, embracing the opportunity and preference to grieve alone.
