  • iMono

  • In London, 1,100 schools are close to roads carrying more than 10,000 vehicles a day, the health of thousands of pupils as well as passer byers are at risk. Air pollution and dirty air is a severe risk to children as they are more prone to harsh illnesses.

    A solution in building a public understanding about our environment. It’s a device which can be worn or fixed onto objects by parents, cyclists, joggers or anyone interested in air quality. It’s designed to allow the recording of data and visualise an invisible world in a way it can benefit everyone. It changes colour (e.g. blue to white) in relation to the amount of high pollution it detects, notifying the user. They can connect the device to a computer via USB cable to view their data and are able to learn more about their environment, themselves and share this information with a local or global community.
